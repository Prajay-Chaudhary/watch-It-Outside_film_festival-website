Rules at Watch It Outside Area

MAKE YOUR NIGHT OUT FUN FOR EVERYONE

~

  1. Keep your trash only in the dustbin, don’t litter.
  2. Don’t talk on the phone after the movie starts.
  3. 10/10 theaters agree that talking on your phone during a movie is a terrible idea.
  4. Please follow all COVID-19 guidelines while attending the Watch It Outside . This includes, but is not limited to, keeping safe social distancing practices ( at least 6′ away from other groups of people ) and the mandatory wearing of face masks when outside of your car.
  5. Have additional questions? Need assistance? Email us at info@watchitoutside.com

Frequently Asked Questions

YOU HAVE QUESTIONS? WE HAVE ANSWERS

~

Where to register?

You can register here or you can find somewhere else.

Do i need to pay while registering?

No, you don’t have pay while registering. The WatchItOutside is a free film festival to watch movies for free.

Is it open to audiances of all ages?

Yes, the Watch It Outside is open to everyone.

What time can I show up?

The gates open 1-hour prior to your movie’s showtime.Feel free to check out the amazing Manhattan WatchItOutside views while you wait, and if you want to snag a pic for your ‘gram tag us @watch_it_outside & use the hashtag #watchitoutside.

Is there assigned seating?

No, there is not. All spots are first-come-first-serve. If you want a specific spot, please make sure to come as early as possible if you want a chance to snag it.

Is there parking area?

Yes, we do have parking area to park your bike,car,bycycle etc for free.

Do you have bathrooms?

Yes, we have proper bathrooms. No one likes porta-potties and we won’t make you use them.

Are pets allowed?

Yes, we’d be happy to have your furry friend on board. Just make sure to clean up after them if you bring them.

Do you play movies while it’s raining or snowing?

No, we won’t play movies during raining or snowing. Just make sure to see the weather conditions before you come.

Do you have subtitles for the hard of hearing?

Yes, though we only have English subtitles for those who are hard of hearing.
